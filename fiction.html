Fiction Books

Lessons in Chemistry by Bonnie Garmus (4.5/5)

I loved this book! Elizabeth Zott was not your average 1960's woman. She was a brilliant chemist who was acutely aware of and intolerant of the rampant sexism in her workplace but did not let that stop her from working toward her goals. It was fascinating but also infuriating being engrossed in her perspective and feeling what she went through as a woman during that time period. She dared other women to change the status quo. The book also made me reflect on the treatment of women today in American society and draw parallels between then and now. I highly recommed this book. Bonus: AppleTV also made it into a show which I recently started. (Trigger warning: Both the book and show include scenes of sexual assault.)

The Midnight Library by Matt Haig (3/5)

This book had a very interesting premise. Between life and death there is a library. When Nora finds herself in the Midnight Library, she has a chance to make things right. Up until this point, her life has been full of unhappiness and regret. But things are about to change. The books in the Midnight Library enable Nora to live as if she had done things differently, undoing each regret and attempting to work out her perfect life. But things aren't always what she imagined they'd be, and soon she is working against the clock. Before time runs out, she must answer the ultimate question: what is the best way to live? I saw so many great things about this book but rated it like I did simply because I felt it was a little slow and didn't live up to the hype in my opinion.
